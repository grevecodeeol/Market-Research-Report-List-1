<p><p>The body sensor market encompasses two primary types: implantable and wearable sensors. Implantable sensors are devices embedded within the body to monitor health metrics like glucose levels or cardiac functions, providing continuous and precise data directly from the source. In contrast, wearable sensors are external devices, such as smartwatches or fitness trackers, that track various health parameters like heart rate, activity levels, and sleep patterns. Both types facilitate personalized healthcare and enhance patient monitoring while contributing to the growing trend of telemedicine and remote health management.</p></p>
