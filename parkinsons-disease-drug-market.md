<p><ul><li>Carbidopa/Levodopa</li><li>Dopamine Receptor Agonists</li><li>MAO-Inhibitors</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Parkinson's disease drug market comprises several key types. Carbidopa/Levodopa, the most commonly prescribed, combines two medications to replenish dopamine levels in the brain. Dopamine receptor agonists mimic dopamine's effects, providing symptom relief while potentially reducing motor fluctuations. MAO-inhibitors block the enzyme that breaks down dopamine, thus prolonging its availability and enhancing its effects. Each category plays a crucial role in managing Parkinson's symptoms, helping to improve the quality of life for patients.</p></p>
