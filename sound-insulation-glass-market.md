<p><ul><li>Hollow Glass</li><li>Vacuum Glass</li><li>Laminated Glass</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The sound insulation glass market comprises three primary types: hollow glass, vacuum glass, and laminated glass. Hollow glass features an air gap between two glass panes, providing effective sound reduction. Vacuum glass utilizes a vacuum space to eliminate sound transmission, offering superior insulation in a thinner profile. Laminated glass consists of layers of glass joined with a sound-dampening interlayer, enhancing acoustic performance. Each type addresses varying sound insulation needs, catering to residential and commercial applications for noise reduction.</p></p>
